Indiana Jones Film Rankings

My Rankings

#5 The Kingdom of the Crystal Skull (2008)

It’s got some of the Indiana Jones magic but it’s not quite the same. It just feels less inspired. Not as bad as the South Park episode leads on.

#4 The Last Crusade (1989)

This one falls off a little bit for me on re-watch. The final act is iconic, and I love Sean Connery, but it all gets a little too hokey for me.

#3 The Dial of Destiny (2023)

Way exceeded my expectations. I don’t get how you can’t love this movie. The voice/body sync was weird, but the prologue is incredible, almost Casino Royale level. The action is fantastic throughout and they use the nostalgia tastefully. I enjoy the way Indy’s goddaughter resembles him but also presents her own unique and complementary persona. There are some plot holes and question marks, and it’s too long. But hey it’s a send off movie and it was a fitting way to honor an iconic American character.

#2 The Temple of Doom (1984)

This was the movie in the original trilogy that seared into my young mind the most. It’s a perfect concoction of evil, presented on a platter for the audience to shudder at. As a 10 year old, I was roped in, and as a 30 year old I still shudder.

#1 Raiders of the Lost Ark (1981)

Rewatching these for the first time in a while, and for the first time ever with our kids - the legend and action of Indiana Jones is top level. Spielberg found a great formula (minus the misogyny), and he built on it well, just as he did with Jurassic Park. I don’t connect with Raiders of the Lost Ark enough to say it’s one of my favorite films. I’m interested in thinking through what Spielberg has to say about the spectrum of faith v fantasy he presents. He seems to be exploring his religious roots in all of these films. But I’m not sure there’s a lot of substance to what he has to say. But I know I’ll be revisiting this story for years to come.
